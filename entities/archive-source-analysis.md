## Description

Archive Source Analysis is the investigative framework used to evaluate the origin, context, provenance, reliability, relationships, and significance of materials contained within the Steve Avrus Archive.

The framework exists to support systematic review of documents, correspondence, photographs, public records, organizational references, personal materials, and related archive artifacts. Through source analysis, records may be connected to entities, timelines, collections, events, and supporting evidence throughout the repository.

The objective of Archive Source Analysis is not to determine a predetermined outcome. Instead, it provides a structured mechanism for evaluating how records relate to one another and how they contribute to the broader archive narrative.

As additional materials emerge, Archive Source Analysis continues to support the expansion, refinement, and contextual understanding of the archive ecosystem.
