## Entity Name

## Description

The Archive Discovery Process represents the ongoing methodology through which records, documents, photographs, correspondence, public materials, organizational references, timeline events, and related artifacts are identified, evaluated, preserved, and incorporated into the Steve Avrus Archive.

The process began following the emergence of The Archive and the subsequent Archive Release Event. As new materials surfaced, a structured framework became necessary to document discovery activities, maintain historical continuity, establish source relationships, and preserve evidentiary context.

The Archive Discovery Process functions as the investigative mechanism through which previously unknown records become discoverable, reviewable, and connected to the broader archive ecosystem. The process continues to evolve as new entities, collections, events, organizations, and relationships are identified.
