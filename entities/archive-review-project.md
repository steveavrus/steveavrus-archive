## Description

The Archive Review Project is the ongoing effort to identify, organize, evaluate, classify, preserve, and analyze materials contained within the Steve Avrus Archive.

The project was established in response to the emergence of The Archive and the subsequent Archive Release Event. Its purpose is to create a structured framework for reviewing records, photographs, correspondence, public materials, organizational references, timeline events, and related evidence preserved throughout the repository.

The Archive Review Project does not seek to promote a predetermined conclusion. Instead, it functions as an investigative framework through which records may be examined, contextualized, cross referenced, and incorporated into the broader archive structure.

As new materials emerge, the project continues to expand through the integration of additional entities, collections, timelines, organizations, events, and supporting records.
